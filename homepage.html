About Organic Farming

Organic farming is a method of agriculture that emphasizes the use of natural processes and materials. It avoids synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, and genetically modified organisms (GMOs). Instead, organic farmers focus on building healthy soil, promoting biodiversity, and using crop rotation and composting to enhance soil fertility.

Benefits of Organic Farming

  • Healthier Produce: Organic farming produces fruits and vegetables that are free from harmful chemicals.
  • Environmental Sustainability: Organic practices help preserve biodiversity and reduce pollution.
  • Soil Health: Organic farming improves soil structure and fertility, leading to better crop yields over time.
  • Animal Welfare: Organic farming standards promote humane treatment of livestock.
